Market Knowledge

Commercial Real Estate Sectors

A working knowledge of the five core CRE asset classes — their investment drivers, supply dynamics, and macroeconomic sensitivities, with a focus on the Toronto market.

🏭
Industrial
Logistics, warehousing & last-mile
Investment Drivers
  • E-commerce growth driving sustained demand for distribution space
  • Near-shoring and supply chain restructuring increasing domestic inventory needs
  • Low vacancy across major markets supporting rent growth
Toronto Market Context
  • GTA is Canada's largest industrial market, anchored by the 400-series highway corridors
  • Availability rates rising as new supply delivers, but demand expected to tighten through 2025
  • Mississauga, Brampton, and Vaughan remain key submarkets for big-box logistics
Key Metrics
  • Net absorption, availability rate, average asking net rent
  • Clear height, truck courts, and dock doors as value drivers
CBRE Insight CBRE's Q4 2025 industrial report noted GTA net absorption of 4.3M SF — the highest of any Canadian market — driven largely by pre-leased new supply. CBRE Toronto's industrial team tracks over 500M SF of GTA inventory.
🏢
Multifamily
Rental apartments & purpose-built residential
Investment Drivers
  • Structural housing undersupply in Toronto driving persistent rental demand
  • High homeownership costs pushing demand into the rental market
  • Immigration-driven population growth sustaining absorption
Toronto Market Context
  • Toronto's purpose-built rental vacancy rate remains near historic lows
  • Rent control regime on pre-1991 buildings creates a two-tier market dynamic
  • Intensification along transit corridors (Eglinton Crosstown, Ontario Line) driving new supply nodes
Key Metrics
  • Vacancy rate, average market rent, rent-to-income ratios
  • NOI growth and cap rate spreads to government bonds
CBRE Insight CBRE's National Investment Team is active in multifamily capital markets across the GTA, tracking purpose-built rental transactions and providing valuation advisory to major pension fund and REIT clients.
🏙️
Office
Workspace, CBD & suburban campuses
Investment Drivers
  • Flight-to-quality driving demand for AAA and well-repositioned Class A product
  • Hybrid work permanently reshaping space requirements per employee
  • Conversion opportunities creating value from distressed Class B/C assets
Toronto Market Context
  • Downtown Toronto recorded 2.65M SF of positive net absorption in 2025 — second consecutive year of recovery
  • AAA vacancy sits at just 7.2%; CIBC Square II (1.5M SF) is the only significant new supply, fully pre-leased
  • Union Station adjacency is the defining location variable — tenants prioritise transit access to drive return-to-office
Key Metrics
  • Availability vs. vacancy rate, net absorption, sublease as % of vacancy
  • Submarket divergence: downtown core vs. Midtown vs. suburban 905
CBRE Insight CBRE's National Investment Team recently advised on the sale of 70 York Street — a 210,000 SF Class A tower in the Financial District — for $134.6M, reflecting a 6.5% cap rate and below-replacement-cost basis of $639/SF.
🛍️
Retail
Neighbourhood centres, malls & mixed-use
Investment Drivers
  • Experiential and necessity-based retail outperforming discretionary categories
  • Grocery-anchored neighbourhood centres demonstrating resilience through cycles
  • Mixed-use intensification unlocking additional value on retail-zoned land
Toronto Market Context
  • Wellness, athleisure, and beauty dominated Toronto retail leasing in 2025, per CBRE research
  • Ossington Avenue emerged as one of the city's most in-demand streetfront retail corridors
  • Simons entering Yorkdale and Eaton Centre signals renewed anchor tenant confidence in major centres
Key Metrics
  • Sales per square foot, occupancy cost ratio, foot traffic counts
  • Anchor tenant credit quality and lease expiry profiles
CBRE Insight CBRE's Urban Retail team, led by SVP Arlin Markowitz, specialises in streetfront and luxury retail investment in Downtown Toronto — one of North America's most active urban retail markets.
🖥️
Data Centers
Digital infrastructure & cloud facilities
Investment Drivers
  • AI and cloud computing driving unprecedented demand for compute capacity
  • Power availability and grid access emerging as the primary site constraint globally
  • Institutional capital repositioning data centers as core infrastructure allocation
Toronto Market Context
  • Toronto is Canada's largest data centre market, benefiting from proximity to US hyperscalers and a stable regulatory environment
  • Power grid constraints in the GTA are increasingly limiting new development — energy access is becoming the key differentiator
  • Canadian data sovereignty concerns driving domestic demand from financial services and government tenants
Key Metrics
  • Megawatts (MW) of critical IT load, power usage effectiveness (PUE)
  • Colocation vs. hyperscale lease structure, utilization rates
CBRE Insight CBRE's Global Data Centre Solutions team tracks Canadian market fundamentals and advises institutional investors on acquisitions, development, and leasing strategy across the digital infrastructure sector.

Hi, I'm Amanda — in my free time I like playing soccer, going to the gym, and my favourite colour is pink. Oh, and I firmly believe an espresso martini is the right drink for any occasion. Now that we've covered the important stuff:

I studied Finance and Economics at Toronto Metropolitan University with a Minor in Real Estate Management. The economics lens stuck with me — markets, behaviour, capital flows, policy. Once you start reading the world that way, it's hard to stop.

My interest in real estate started before university. My family lost our home during the 2008 housing crisis, and even as a kid I understood that real estate isn't just an asset class — it has real consequences for real people. That's stayed with me, and it's a big part of why commercial real estate feels like the right place to build a career.

I currently work at CBRE Sydney, where I've had a front-row seat to how occupiers engage with institutional-scale commercial space. In mid-2026, I'm relocating back to Toronto to move into research and capital markets — the work I've been building toward.

On the technical side: I'm completing the A.CRE Accelerator to sharpen my financial modelling in Excel, and I'm genuinely AI-fluent — this site was built using AI, and so were some of the models you'll find here. But I've also built those same models cell by cell and that's the point — knowing why the model works means you can build it any way you need to.
