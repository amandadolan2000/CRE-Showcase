01 — Executive Summary

CBRE's National Investment Team is pleased to present 70 York Street, an institutionally owned and managed Class A office tower situated in the heart of Toronto's Financial District. The Property represents a rare opportunity to acquire a stabilized, income-producing office asset at a basis well below replacement cost in Canada's premier office market.

The Property was comprehensively repositioned by the current ownership over the past three years, including significant capital investment in common areas, building systems, and tenant improvements. This program of work has driven occupancy from distressed levels to 92.5%, with a diversified tenancy anchored by federal government covenant (CMHC) and complemented by a deep mix of financial services and real estate firms.

The offering is structured as an off-market process, with qualified purchasers invited to submit offers on or before the stated bid date. CBRE and TD Cornerstone Commercial Realty are acting as co-advisors to the Vendor.

Analyst Note (A. Dolan): This transaction is being brought to market at an inflection point for Toronto's downtown office sector. With AAA vacancy at just 7.2% and two consecutive years of positive net absorption, well-located, renovated Class A product is in genuine scarcity. At $639/SF and a 6.5% going-in cap rate, the pricing reflects a meaningful discount to replacement cost while offering institutional-quality income from day one.

Investment Thesis

Toronto's downtown office market is in a genuine recovery cycle, with two consecutive years of positive net absorption and the bulk of pandemic-era sublease space absorbed. The flight-to-quality dynamic is bifurcating the market sharply — AAA and well-repositioned Class A product is effectively full, while Class B/C continues to soften.

70 York sits at the intersection of these two dynamics: a renovated, stabilized asset in a AAA location, trading at a Class A cap rate. The CMHC anchor provides defensive income while the remaining tenancy mix offers embedded rent growth as below-market leases roll to market over the hold period.

For institutional capital seeking core Canadian office exposure, this offering represents one of the few opportunities to acquire a fully freehold, stabilized asset in the Financial District at a below-replacement-cost basis.
