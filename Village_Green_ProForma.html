Investment Thesis Commentary

Acquisition Rationale: Village Green represents a stabilised, large-format institutional multifamily acquisition in one of Toronto's most supply-constrained residential corridors. At $374,894/door, Brookfield acquired at a meaningful discount to replacement cost (estimated $500,000–$600,000/door for new-build in the submarket), with embedded upside through mark-to-market on suite turnover.

Market Context: Toronto's purpose-built rental market entered 2024 in a near-unprecedented supply/demand imbalance. Population growth driven by immigration targets (400,000+ per year, since recalibrated) and the near-absence of new PBR completions below Bloor kept vacancy at approximately 2.5%. The going-in cap rate of ~4.50% is consistent with CBRE's tracked range of 4.25%–4.75% for stabilised institutional multifamily in inner Toronto.

Underwriting Conservatism: Our model applies a 25 bps exit cap expansion (4.50% → 5.00%), a 3.0% annual rent growth over Years 1–5 (below recent actuals of 3–6%), and a conventional 65% LTV structure rather than CMHC MLI Select, which would have allowed >90% LTV. The levered IRR of approximately 9.4% represents an adequate risk-adjusted return above the CBRE unlevered target of 6.25%, with meaningful downside protection at the acquisition basis.

Key Risks: Interest rate environment; provincial rent control constraints on existing tenancies; immigration policy recalibration reducing near-term demand tailwinds; potential suite renovation CapEx above reserve levels; financing risk at refinance (Year 10 assumes full exit).
